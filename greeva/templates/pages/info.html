What is Hydroponics?

Definition

Hydroponics is a method of growing plants without soil, using mineral nutrient solutions in a water solvent. Plants receive nutrients directly through their root systems, allowing for precise control over growing conditions.

Why Use Hydroponics?

Hydroponics enables year-round cultivation, uses 90% less water than traditional farming, requires less space, and provides faster growth rates with higher yields.

Key Benefits

No soil-borne diseases, precise nutrient control, reduced pesticide use, consistent crop quality, and the ability to grow in urban or non-arable areas.

Types of Hydroponic Systems

NFT (Nutrient Film Technique)

A thin film of nutrient solution continuously flows over plant roots in a sloped channel.

Efficient Low Water Use
Best for: Leafy greens, herbs
DWC (Deep Water Culture)

Plant roots are suspended in oxygenated nutrient solution with constant aeration.

Simple Fast Growth
Best for: Lettuce, spinach, herbs
Aeroponics

Plant roots hang in air and are misted with nutrient solution at regular intervals.

Advanced Maximum Oxygen
Best for: High-value crops
Drip System

Nutrient solution is dripped onto the base of each plant through small tubes.

Versatile Scalable
Best for: Tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers
Wick System

Passive system using wicks to draw nutrient solution from reservoir to growing medium.

Passive Low Maintenance
Best for: Small plants, herbs
Ebb and Flow

Growing tray is periodically flooded with nutrient solution, then drained back to reservoir.

Flexible Reliable
Best for: Various crops

Core Components

Water Reservoir

Holds nutrient solution and provides a stable environment for plant roots. Size depends on system scale and plant water requirements.

Grow Lights

Provide essential light spectrum for photosynthesis. LED lights are energy-efficient and can be customized for different growth stages.

Water Pumps

Circulate nutrient solution throughout the system. Essential for maintaining proper flow and oxygenation.

Sensors (pH, EC, Temperature)

Monitor critical parameters in real-time. Essential for maintaining optimal growing conditions and preventing issues.

Nutrient Solutions

Carefully balanced mixtures of essential minerals. Different formulations exist for vegetative and flowering stages.

Air Pumps & Stones

Oxygenate the nutrient solution, preventing root rot and promoting healthy root development.

Setup & Monitoring Basics

Initial Setup

1. System Assembly: Install reservoir, pumps, and growing containers according to system type.

2. Nutrient Preparation: Mix nutrient solution following manufacturer guidelines.

3. pH Adjustment: Balance pH to optimal range (5.5-6.5) before adding plants.

Sensor Calibration

pH Sensors: Calibrate using standard buffer solutions (pH 4.0, 7.0, 10.0).

EC Sensors: Use calibration solution (typically 1413 μS/cm).

Temperature: Verify against known temperature reference.

Monitoring Best Practices

Daily: Check pH, EC, water level, and plant health.

Weekly: Clean filters, inspect pumps, check for algae growth.

Monthly: Deep clean system, recalibrate sensors, replace solution.

Understanding Sensor Data

pH Level
5.5 - 6.5
Optimal range for nutrient absorption. Below 5.5 is acidic, above 6.5 is alkaline.
EC (Electrical Conductivity)
1.2 - 2.0 mS/cm
Indicates nutrient concentration. Higher EC = more nutrients in solution.
Water Temperature
18 - 22°C
Affects oxygen levels and nutrient uptake. Too warm reduces dissolved oxygen.
Humidity
50 - 70%
Affects transpiration rate. Too low causes stress, too high risks mold.
Why These Values Matter

pH: Controls nutrient availability. Each nutrient has an optimal pH range for absorption. Incorrect pH locks out nutrients even if present.

EC: Indicates total dissolved salts. Too low means insufficient nutrients; too high can burn roots and inhibit water uptake.

Temperature: Affects metabolic rates, oxygen solubility, and pathogen growth. Consistent temperature promotes stable growth.

Humidity: Influences transpiration, which drives nutrient uptake. Proper humidity prevents stress and disease.
